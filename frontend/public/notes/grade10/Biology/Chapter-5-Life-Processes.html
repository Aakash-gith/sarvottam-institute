What are Life Processes?

Life processes are the activities that all living organisms perform to maintain life and survive. These processes are essential for growth, reproduction, response to environment, and maintenance of homeostasis (stable internal conditions).

Nutrition

Nutrition is the process of taking in food and using it for growth, energy, and body maintenance. It involves ingestion, digestion, absorption, and assimilation.

  • Autotrophic Nutrition: Organisms make their own food using simple inorganic substances
    • Green plants (Photosynthesis)
    • Some bacteria (Chemosynthesis)
  • Heterotrophic Nutrition: Organisms obtain food from others
    • Holozoic: Ingesting whole organisms (most animals)
    • Parasitic: Living on/in host organisms
    • Saprophytic: Feeding on dead organic matter (fungi, decomposers)

Photosynthesis

The process by which green plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose (food) and oxygen. Occurs mainly in the leaves.

Photosynthetic Equation:
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Two stages:
1. Light Reaction (in thylakoid): Light energy converted to chemical energy (ATP, NADPH)
2. Dark Reaction/Calvin Cycle (in stroma): CO₂ converted to glucose using ATP and NADPH

Respiration

The process of breaking down glucose to release energy in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate). The energy released is used for all life activities.

  • Aerobic Respiration: Occurs in the presence of oxygen
    C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy (38 ATP)
    Occurs in: Glycolysis (cytoplasm), Krebs Cycle (mitochondria), ETC (mitochondria)
  • Anaerobic Respiration: Occurs without oxygen (in muscles during intense exercise)
    C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2 Lactic Acid + Energy (2 ATP)
    or
    C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2 Ethanol + 2CO₂ + Energy (2 ATP) [in plants and yeast]

Digestion

The process of breaking down complex food into simpler substances that can be absorbed and used by the body.

  • Mechanical Digestion: Breaking food into smaller pieces (chewing, churning)
  • Chemical Digestion: Breaking chemical bonds using enzymes
    • Carbohydrates → Monosaccharides (by amylase)
    • Proteins → Amino acids (by protease/pepsin)
    • Fats → Glycerol and fatty acids (by lipase)

Absorption

The process of movement of digested food (nutrients) from the small intestine into the blood. Small intestine has many villi (finger-like projections) to increase surface area for absorption.

Assimilation

The process of utilization of absorbed nutrients for growth, energy, and repair of body tissues.

Transportation

Movement of materials (nutrients, oxygen, hormones, waste) within organisms.

  • In Plants:
    • Xylem: Transports water and minerals upward from roots
    • Phloem: Transports glucose and other organic substances in all directions
  • In Animals:
    • Circulatory System: Heart, blood vessels, blood
    • Lymphatic System: Lymph vessels and nodes

Excretion

Removal of waste products produced during metabolism from the body. Essential for maintaining homeostasis.

  • In Humans: Urea is main nitrogenous waste
    • Kidneys: Filter blood and produce urine
    • Lungs: Eliminate CO₂
    • Skin: Eliminate water and salts through sweat
  • In Plants:
    • Leaves: Release O₂ and CO₂ through stomata
    • Roots: Release salts and other substances

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Life processes: Nutrition, Respiration, Transportation, Excretion
  • ✓ Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • ✓ Aerobic respiration uses oxygen
  • ✓ Transportation moves nutrients and oxygen
