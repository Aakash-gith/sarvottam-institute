Chapter 1 - Chemical Reactions and Equations

What is a Chemical Reaction?

A chemical reaction is a process where one or more substances (reactants) are transformed into different substances (products). In a chemical reaction, bonds are broken and new bonds are formed.

Characteristics of a Chemical Reaction:
  • Change in color
  • Evolution of gas
  • Change in temperature
  • Formation of precipitate
  • Change in physical state

Types of Chemical Reactions

  • Combination Reaction (A + B → AB): Two or more substances combine to form a single product. Example: C + O₂ → CO₂
  • Decomposition Reaction (AB → A + B): A single substance breaks down into two or more products. Example: 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂ (with catalyst)
  • Displacement Reaction (AB + C → AC + B): An element displaces another element from a compound. Example: Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu
  • Double Displacement Reaction (AB + CD → AD + CB): Two compounds exchange ions to form new compounds. Example: AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl↓ + NaNO₃
  • Redox Reaction: Involves transfer of electrons between substances

Chemical Equations

A chemical equation is a representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulas and symbols. It shows the reactants on the left and products on the right.

General Form:
Reactants → Products
Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Symbols Used:
→ indicates formation of products
↓ indicates precipitate formation
↑ indicates gas evolution
(s) - solid, (l) - liquid, (g) - gas, (aq) - aqueous

Balancing Chemical Equations

In a balanced equation, the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides. This is done by adding coefficients (not changing subscripts).

Steps to Balance an Equation:
  • List the number of each type of atom on both sides
  • Find atoms that are not balanced
  • Adjust coefficients to balance
  • Check that all atoms are balanced
Law of Conservation of Mass: Matter is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction

Oxidation and Reduction (Redox Reactions)

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons or gain of oxygen (or loss of hydrogen)
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons or loss of oxygen (or gain of hydrogen)
  • Oxidizing Agent: Substance that gets reduced (gains electrons)
  • Reducing Agent: Substance that gets oxidized (loses electrons)
Example: In the reaction Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu
Zn is oxidized (loses 2 electrons)
Cu²⁺ is reduced (gains 2 electrons)
Zn is the reducing agent, Cu²⁺ is the oxidizing agent

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Chemical reactions transform substances
  • ✓ Four types of chemical reactions
  • ✓ Chemical equations must be balanced
  • ✓ Oxidation and reduction occur together
