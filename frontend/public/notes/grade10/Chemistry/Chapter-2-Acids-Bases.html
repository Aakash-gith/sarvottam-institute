Chapter 2 - Acids, Bases and Salts

Class X Chemistry | CBSE

Acids

Acids are compounds that produce H⁺ ions (or H₃O⁺) in aqueous solution. They have sour taste and are corrosive.

  • Strong Acids: HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃ (completely ionize in water)
  • Weak Acids: Acetic acid (CH₃COOH), Citric acid (partially ionize in water)
  • pH range: < 7
  • Indicator Color: Turn blue litmus paper red

Bases

Bases are compounds that produce OH⁻ ions in aqueous solution. They have bitter taste and feel slippery.

  • Strong Bases: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂ (completely ionize in water)
  • Weak Bases: Ammonia (NH₃, partially ionize in water)
  • pH range: > 7
  • Indicator Color: Turn red litmus paper blue

pH Scale

pH = -log[H⁺]
pOH = -log[OH⁻]
pH + pOH = 14

pH Classification:
pH < 7: Acidic solution
pH = 7: Neutral solution (at 25°C)
pH > 7: Basic/Alkaline solution

Indicators

Indicators are organic compounds that change color in acidic or basic solutions.

  • Litmus: Red in acidic, blue in basic
  • Methyl Orange: Red in acidic (pH < 3.1), orange in neutral, yellow in basic
  • Phenolphthalein: Colorless in acidic (pH < 8.2), pink in basic
  • Methyl Red: Red in acidic, yellow in basic

Neutralization Reaction

When an acid reacts with a base, they neutralize each other to produce salt and water.

General Neutralization:
Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Example:
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
H₂SO₄ + 2KOH → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O

Salts

Salts are ionic compounds formed when an acid reacts with a base. They conduct electricity in aqueous solution.

  • Normal Salts: Formed from complete neutralization (NaCl, KNO₃)
  • Acidic Salts: Formed from partial neutralization of polybasic acids (NaHSO₄)
  • Basic Salts: Formed from partial neutralization of polyacidic bases

Properties of Acids and Bases

Property Acids Bases
Taste Sour Bitter
Texture Corrosive Slippery
Litmus Paper Red litmus → stays red Red litmus → turns blue
Phenolphthalein Colorless Pink

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Acids produce H⁺ ions (pH < 7)
  • ✓ Bases produce OH⁻ ions (pH > 7)
  • ✓ Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • ✓ Indicators detect acids and bases
