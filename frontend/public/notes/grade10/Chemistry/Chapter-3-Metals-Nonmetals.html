Chapter 3 - Metals and Non-metals

Class X Chemistry | CBSE

Metals

Metals are elements that form positive ions by losing electrons. They have unique characteristic properties that make them valuable for various applications.

  • Lustrous (Shiny): Reflect light, used in mirrors and decorative items
  • Good conductors of electricity: Used in wires and electronic devices
  • Good conductors of heat: Used in utensils and cooking appliances
  • Malleable: Can be hammered into thin sheets (example: gold foil)
  • Ductile: Can be drawn into wires
  • High melting and boiling points: Tungsten (3422°C), Iron (1538°C)
  • Sonorous: Produce sound when struck (bell metal)
  • Solid at room temperature: Except mercury (liquid)
Examples of Metals: Iron, Copper, Aluminum, Gold, Silver, Zinc, Lead, Tin, Magnesium

Non-metals

Non-metals are elements that form negative ions by gaining electrons. They have properties opposite to metals.

  • Non-lustrous (Dull): Do not reflect light well
  • Poor conductors of electricity: Except graphite and iodine
  • Poor conductors of heat: Good insulators
  • Brittle: Break easily when hammered
  • Low melting and boiling points: Iodine (184°C), Sulfur (445°C)
  • Non-sonorous: Do not produce sound
  • Various physical states: Solid, liquid, or gas at room temperature
Examples of Non-metals: Oxygen, Nitrogen, Carbon, Sulfur, Phosphorus, Chlorine, Iodine, Hydrogen, Noble gases

Chemical Reactivity of Metals

Metals react with oxygen, water, and acids. The reactivity depends on their position in the periodic table.

  • With Oxygen: 4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O (Sodium burns in air)
  • With Water: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂↑ (Sodium reacts vigorously)
  • With Acids: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂↑

Reactivity Series of Metals

The order of reactivity of common metals (most reactive to least reactive):

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au

Key Points:
• A more reactive metal can displace a less reactive metal from its compound
• Example: Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu (Iron displaces copper)

Displacement Reactions

In a displacement reaction, a more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its salt solution.

General Form: A + BC → AC + B
Example:
Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu
Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

Comparison of Metals and Non-metals

Property Metals Non-metals
Appearance Lustrous/shiny Dull
Electrical Conductivity Excellent Poor (except graphite)
Thermal Conductivity Good Poor
Malleability Malleable Brittle
Ductility Ductile Not ductile
Melting Point Generally high Generally low
Density Generally high Generally low
Ionization Form cations (positive ions) Form anions (negative ions)

Corrosion

Corrosion is the process of destruction of metals due to reaction with atmospheric gases, moisture, or chemicals.

  • Rusting of Iron: Formation of rust (hydrated iron oxide) on iron surface
  • Prevention: Painting, oiling, galvanization, alloying

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Metals: Good conductors, malleable, ductile
  • ✓ Non-metals: Poor conductors, brittle
  • ✓ Reactivity series determines displacement
  • ✓ Metals form cations; non-metals form anions
