Activity 13.1

Designing an Aquarium

Understanding artificial ecosystems.

  • Select a large jar or tank. Fill it with water.
  • Add pebbles and oxygen pump (aerator).
  • Add algae and aquatic plants (Producers).
  • Add small fish (Consumers).
Analysis

An aquarium is a **self-sustaining artificial ecosystem** ONLY if maintained. Unlike a pond, it lacks natural decomposers, so we must clean it to prevent toxic ammonia buildup from fish waste.

Activity 13.4

Ozone Layer Study

Tracking the Ozone Hole.

  • Visit the library or internet to find out about Ozone Depletion chemicals.
  • Research how the size of the Ozone hole has changed in recent years.
Observation

The main culprits are **CFCs** (Chlorofluorocarbons). Since the **Montreal Protocol (1987)**, the production of CFCs has dropped, and the ozone layer has started to recover slowly.

Activity 13.5

Biodegradable vs Non-Biodegradable

Decomposition experiment.

  • Collect kitchen waste (peels, bread), paper, plastic, glass, and metal scraps.
  • Burry them in a pit or pot (approx 15 cm deep).
  • Dampen with water. Leave for 15 days.
  • Dig up and observe.
Observation

**Changed:** Peels, bread, paper (Rotten/Decomposed). These are **Biodegradable**.
**Unchanged:** Plastic, glass, metal. These are **Non-biodegradable** and persist in soil.
