NCERT Activities

Chapter 5: Life Processes

Activity 5.1

Chlorophyll is Essential for Photosynthesis

Procedure
  • Take a potted plant with variegated leaves (e.g., Money plant or Crotons).
  • Keep it in a dark room for 3 days to destarch it.
  • Keep it in sunlight for 6 hours.
  • Pluck a leaf, mark green areas, boil in water, then in alcohol (water bath).
  • Wash and dip in Iodine solution.
Observation & Conclusion

Only the green parts of the leaf (which contained chlorophyll) turn blue-black with iodine. The non-green parts remain colorless/brown. This proves that Chlorophyll is necessary for photosynthesis (starch formation).

Activity 5.2

CO₂ is Essential for Photosynthesis

Procedure
  • Take two healthy potted plants. Keep in dark for 3 days.
  • Place separate glass plates. Place a watch-glass with Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) near one plant (Plant A).
  • Cover both with bell jars and seal with vaseline.
  • Keep in sunlight for 2 hours. Test leaves for starch with Iodine.
Observation & Conclusion

Leaf from Plant A (with KOH) does not turn blue-black. Leaf from Plant B turns blue-black. KOH absorbs CO₂. Lack of starch in Plant A proves that Carbon Dioxide is necessary for photosynthesis.

Activity 5.3

Action of Saliva on Starch

Procedure
  • Take 1ml starch solution in two test tubes (A and B).
  • Add 1ml saliva to tube A. Leave both for 20 mins.
  • Add dilute Iodine solution to both.
Observation & Conclusion

Tube B (Starch only) turns blue-black. Tube A (Starch + Saliva) does NOT turn blue-black. This shows that saliva contains an enzyme (Salivary Amylase) that breaks down starch into sugar.

Activity 5.4

CO₂ is Released During Respiration

Procedure
  • Take some freshly prepared lime water in a test tube.
  • Blow air into it through a straw.
  • Note how long it takes to turn milky. Compare with air passed via syringe.
Observation & Conclusion

Lime water turns milky much faster when air is blown from the mouth compared to atmospheric air. This proves that exhaled air contains a higher percentage of Carbon Dioxide.

Activity 5.5

Transpiration in Plants

Procedure
  • Take a healthy potted plant and cover a leaf/branch with a plastic bag.
  • Place in sunlight.
Observation & Conclusion

Droplets of water appear on the inner side of the plastic bag. This shows that plants lose water in the form of vapor through stomata, a process called Transpiration.
