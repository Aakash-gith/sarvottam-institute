Chapter 6: Control and Coordination

Activity 6.1

Role of Smell (Olfactory Receptors) in Taste

Procedure
  • Take some sugar crystals and taste them. Note the taste.
  • Now block your nose by pressing it with your thumb and index finger.
  • Eat the sugar crystals again. Is there a difference in taste?
  • While eating lunch, block your nose similarly and observe if the food taste changes.
Observation & Conclusion

When the nose is blocked, the ability to fully appreciate the flavor of food decreases. This is because flavor is a combination of taste (tongue) and smell (nose). Our olfactory receptors play a major role in identifying flavors.

Activity 6.2

Phototropism in Plants

Procedure
  • Fill a conical flask with water.
  • Cover the neck of the flask with a wire mesh.
  • Keep two or three freshly germinated bean seeds on the wire mesh.
  • Take a cardboard box which is open from one side.
  • Keep the flask in the box in such a manner that the open side of the box faces light coming from a window.
  • Observe after 2-3 days.
  • Now, turn the flask so that the shoots are away from light and roots towards light. Leave it for a few days.
Observation & Conclusion

Initially, shoots grow towards the light. After rotating, the old parts remain as they were, but new growth directions change: shoots bend towards the light (Phototropism) and roots bend away from light (Negative Phototropism).

Activity 6.3

Identifying Endocrine Glands

Procedure
  • Look at the diagram of the endocrine system in the textbook (Figure 6.7).
  • Identify the glands present in the diagram.
  • List the glands associated with different regions of the body (Head, Neck, Chest, Abdomen, etc.).
Observation & Conclusion

The endocrine glands are located in specific regions:

  • HEAD: Pineal gland, Pituitary gland, Hypothalamus
  • NECK: Thyroid, Parathyroid
  • CHEST: Thymus
  • ABDOMEN: Pancreas, Adrenal glands
  • PELVIS: Ovaries (Female), Testes (Male)
Activity 6.4

Hormones and Their Functions

Procedure
  • Prepare a table to identify the hormones secreted by endocrine glands and their functions.
  • Consult the textbook, teachers, or library resources to complete the specific functions.
Observation & Conclusion

Hormones regulate critical body functions:

  • Growth Hormone: Regulates body height and development.
  • Insulin: Lowers blood sugar levels.
  • Testosterone/Oestrogen: Control puberty and reproductive features.
  • Thyroxin: Controls metabolism of carbs, fats, and proteins.
  • Adrenaline: Emergency response (heart rate, breathing).
