Activity 7.1

Binary Fission in Amoeba

Observing permanent slides of asexual reproduction.

Procedure
  • Observe a permanent slide of Amoeba under a microscope.
  • Look for stages where the nucleus is elongating or dividing.
  • Observe another slide showing the separation of daughter cells.
Observation & Conclusion

You will see a single parent cell dividing into two equal-sized daughter cells. First, the nucleus divides (karyokinesis), followed by the division of the cytoplasm (cytokinesis). This confirms Binary Fission.

Activity 7.4

Budding in Yeast/Hydra

Observing outgrowth formation.

Procedure
  • Take some yeast powder and dissolve it in sugar solution. Keep it warm.
  • After an hour, put a drop on a slide and observe.
  • Alternatively, observe a permanent slide of Hydra.
Observation & Conclusion

In Yeast, you may see small bud-like outgrowths forming chains. In Hydra, you will see a small bulge on the parent body (bud) which develops tentacles and eventually detaches. This is Budding.

Activity 7.7

Germination of a Seed

Understanding the parts of a seed (Gram/Chana).

Procedure
  • Soak a few seeds of Bengal gram (chana) or chickpea overnight.
  • Drain the excess water and cover the seeds with a wet cloth for a day.
  • Carefully open the seed (split it along the cotyledons) and observe.
Observation & Conclusion

Upon opening, you will see two thick Cotyledons (food store). Between them is the embryo axis:
• The lower part is the Radicle (future root).
• The upper part is the Plumule (future shoot).
