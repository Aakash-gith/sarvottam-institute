7.1 Basics of Reproduction

Do Organisms Create Copy?

Reproduction involves creating copies of the blueprints of body design.

DNA Copying & Variation

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) in the cell nucleus is the information source for making proteins. If the information is changed, different proteins will be made, leading to altered body designs.

  • Mechanism: Cells build two copies of DNA and split into two cells.
  • Accuracy: No bio-chemical reaction is 100% reliable. Occasional errors during DNA copying lead to variations.
Importance of Variation Variations are useful for the survival of species over time. If a niche (habitat) changes drastically (e.g., temperature rise), variants resistant to that change will survive and reproduce, preventing extinction.
7.2 Modes of Reproduction

Asexual Reproduction

Reproduction involving a single parent.

1. Fission

Parent cell divides into daughter cells.

  • Binary Fission: Cell divides into two (e.g., Amoeba, Leishmania).
  • Multiple Fission: Single cell divides into many daughter cells (e.g., Plasmodium).

2. Fragmentation & Regeneration

Fragmentation: Examples like Spirogyra break into pieces, and each piece grows into a new individual.

Regeneration: If organisms like Hydra or Planaria are cut, specialized cells proliferate and differentiate to reform the whole organism.

3. Budding

In Hydra, a bud develops as an outgrowth due to repeated cell division at one specific site. These buds develop into tiny individuals and detach.

4. Vegetative Propagation

New plants produced from roots, stems, leaves (e.g., Rose, Sugarcane, Bryophyllum).

7.3 Sexual Reproduction

Reproduction in Flowering Plants

Sexual reproduction involves two parents and fusion of gametes (Germ cells).

Types of Flowers

  • Bisexual Flower: Both male and female reproductive parts are present. (e.g., Hibiscus, Mustard).
  • Unisexual Flower: Either male or female reproductive part is present. (e.g., Papaya, Watermelon).

Flower Parts

  • Stamen (Male): Produces Pollen grains (yellowish). Consists of Filament and Anther.
  • Pistil/Carpel (Female): Stigma (sticky top), Style (tube), Ovary (base containing ovules).

Process of Seed Formation

  • Pollination: Transfer of pollen from Stamen to Stigma by air, water, or animals.
  • Fertilisation:
    • Pollen tube grows through style to reach ovary.
    • Male germ cell fuses with female germ cell (Egg).
    • Forms Zygote inside the ovule.
  • Post-Fertilisation:
    • Zygote divides to form Embryo.
    • Ovule develops thick coat and becomes Seed.
    • Ovary ripens to become Fruit (petals fall off).
Double Fertilisation (Key Concept)
In flowering plants, two fusions occur:
  1. Syngamy: Male Gamete (n) + Female Gamete (n) → Zygote (2n) → Embryo.
  2. Triple Fusion: Male Gamete (n) + Secondary Nucleus (2n) → Endosperm Nucleus (3n) → Endosperm (food for embryo).

Female Reproductive System

  • Ovary: Two almond-shaped organs.
    • Produce Egg (one released every month).
    • Secrete Oestrogen / Progesterone.
  • Fallopian Tube (Oviduct): Carries egg from ovary to uterus. Site of Fertilisation.
  • Uterus: Elastic bag-like structure where baby develops. Opens into vagina via cervix.

Scenario A: Egg is Fertilised

  • Zygote is formed in Fallopian tube.
  • It divides and implants in the uterine wall.

Scenario B: Egg is NOT Fertilised

  • The uterine lining (thick & spongy) is not needed.
  • Lining breaks and comes out through vagina as Blood and Mucus.
  • This cycle takes roughly 28 days -> Menstruation.
  • Usually lasts for 2-8 days.

Reproductive Health

Contraception Methods

  • Mechanical Barriers: Condoms (prevent STDs too), Diaphragm.
  • Chemical: Oral Pills (change hormonal balance so eggs are not released).
  • IUCD: Copper-T (placed in Uterus to prevent implantation).
  • Surgical: Vasectomy (Vas deferens blocked), Tubectomy (Fallopian tube blocked).

STDs (Sexually Transmitted Diseases)

  • Bacterial: Gonorrhoea, Syphilis.
  • Viral: Warts, HIV-AIDS.
  • Prevention: Using condoms protects against many infections.
Summary

What You Have Learnt

  • Reproduction creates new individuals from existing ones.
  • Asexual reproduction involves one parent (Fission, Fragmentation, Regeneration, Budding, Spore Formation, Vegetative Propagation).
  • Sexual reproduction involves two parents and allows for more variation.
  • In flowering plants, fertilisation happens in the ovary after pollination.
  • In humans, testes produce sperm/testosterone; ovaries produce eggs/oestrogen.
  • Contraceptive methods avoid pregnancy and prevent STDs.
