Introduction to Reproduction

Reproduction is the biological process that produces new organisms of the same species. It's essential for species survival and contributes to genetic diversity (in sexual reproduction).

Types of Reproduction

Asexual Reproduction Sexual Reproduction
One parent involved Two parents involved
Produces identical offspring (clones) Produces genetically different offspring
No gametes involved Gametes (sperm, egg) fuse
Fast reproduction Slower but more genetic variation
Examples: Bacteria, Hydra, Planaria Examples: Most animals and plants

Asexual Reproduction

1. Binary Fission (Bacteria, Amoeba)

Process: Single cell divides into two identical daughter cells
  • DNA replication
  • Cell elongation
  • Separation of nuclei
  • Cell division
Result: Two genetically identical organisms

2. Budding (Hydra, Yeast)

  • Small outgrowth (bud) develops on parent body
  • Bud develops into new organism
  • Detaches from parent when mature
  • Example: Hydra reproduces by budding

3. Fragmentation (Planaria, Starfish)

  • Body breaks into fragments
  • Each fragment develops into new organism
  • Requires regeneration capability
  • Example: Planaria divides transversely

4. Vegetative Propagation (Plants)

  • Runners: Horizontal stems (Strawberry)
  • Rhizomes: Underground stems (Ginger, Turmeric)
  • Tubers: Underground storage stems (Potato)
  • Bulbs: Underground bud structures (Onion, Garlic)
  • Corms: Compressed stem structures (Gladiolus)
  • Leaf cuttings: New plants from leaves (Begonia)

Sexual Reproduction

Gamete Formation

Gametes: Sex cells (sperm and egg) that fuse during fertilization
  • Sperm: Male gamete (mobile, many produced)
  • Egg/Ovum: Female gamete (stationary, fewer produced)
  • Fertilization: Fusion of sperm and egg to form zygote

Spermatogenesis (Sperm Formation)

  • Site: Seminiferous tubules of testes
  • Starting cell: Spermatogonium (diploid)
  • Process:
    • Mitotic divisions of spermatogonium
    • Meiotic divisions (Meiosis I and II)
    • Spermiogenesis (development into mature sperm)
  • Result: Four functional haploid sperm cells
  • Duration: ~74 days in humans

Oogenesis (Egg Formation)

  • Site: Germinal epithelium of ovaries
  • Starting cell: Oogonium (diploid)
  • Process:
    • Mitotic divisions of oogonium
    • Primary oocyte (diploid) enters Meiosis I
    • First polar body and secondary oocyte formed
    • Secondary oocyte enters Meiosis II (stops at metaphase)
    • Completes Meiosis II after fertilization
  • Result: One functional egg and 2-3 polar bodies

Female Reproductive System

  • Ovaries: Produce eggs and hormones (estrogen, progesterone)
  • Fallopian Tubes: Carry egg from ovary, site of fertilization
  • Uterus: Receives and nourishes zygote/embryo
  • Cervix: Lower part of uterus, opening to vagina
  • Vagina: Birth canal, receives sperm

Male Reproductive System

  • Testes: Produce sperm and testosterone
  • Epididymis: Stores and matures sperm
  • Vas Deferens: Transports sperm
  • Seminal Vesicles: Produce seminal fluid
  • Prostate Gland: Secretes fluids to activate sperm
  • Penis: Delivers sperm into female reproductive tract

Menstrual Cycle (Humans)

The menstrual cycle is a monthly process in females involving hormone changes and uterine changes.

Phases (28 days average):
  • Menstruation (Days 1-5): Shedding of uterine lining, bleeding
  • Follicular Phase (Days 1-13): FSH increases, follicle grows, estrogen increases
  • Ovulation (Day 14): LH surge, egg released from ovary
  • Luteal Phase (Days 15-28): Corpus luteum produces progesterone, prepares uterus for implantation

Fertilization and Development

  • Fertilization: Sperm nucleus fuses with egg nucleus in fallopian tube
    • Restores diploid number (2n)
    • Forms zygote
  • Cleavage: Rapid mitotic divisions form morula then blastocyst
  • Implantation: Blastocyst embeds in uterine wall (6-10 days after fertilization)
  • Placenta Formation: Provides nutrition and oxygen to fetus

Human Gestation and Birth

  • Duration: ~280 days (40 weeks) or 9 months
  • Trimesters:
    • First (Weeks 1-12): Organ formation
    • Second (Weeks 13-27): Rapid growth
    • Third (Weeks 28-40): Fetal maturation, preparation for birth
  • Birth Process: Labor contractions, rupture of amniotic sac, delivery

Puberty Changes

Puberty is the period of rapid growth and sexual maturation.

Female Changes Male Changes

Contraception

Methods to prevent unwanted pregnancy:

  • Barrier Methods: Condoms, diaphragm
  • Hormonal Methods: Pills, patches, injections
  • Intrauterine: IUD
  • Permanent: Tubal ligation, vasectomy
  • Natural: Rhythm method
