Activity 8.1

Variations: Earlobes

Observing variations in a population.

Procedure
  • Observe the ears of all the students in the class.
  • Prepare a list of students having free (detached) earlobes and those having attached earlobes.
  • Calculate the percentage of students having each type of earlobe.
  • Compare this with their parents' earlobes (by asking the students).
Observation & Conclusion

You will find that most people have free earlobes (Dominant trait), while fewer have attached earlobes (Recessive trait). This shows that variation exists even within a single species (humans) and that these traits are inherited.

Activity 8.2

Simulating Mendel's Ratios

Understanding probability in inheritance.

Procedure
  • Take two coins. Let Heads represent Dominant Allele (T) and Tails represent Recessive Allele (t).
  • Toss both coins simultaneously 100 times.
  • Record the combinations: Head-Head (TT), Head-Tail (Tt), and Tail-Tail (tt).
  • Calculate the ratio of the three combinations.
Observation & Conclusion

You should get approximately:
• 25% Head-Head (TT - Pure Tall)
• 50% Head-Tail (Tt - Hybrid Tall)
• 25% Tail-Tail (tt - Dwarf)

This experimentally verifies Mendel's Genotypic Ratio of 1:2:1. Since TT and Tt both appear Tall, the Phenotypic Ratio is 3:1.
