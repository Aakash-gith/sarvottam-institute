Activity 11.1

Verification of Ohm’s Law

Aim: To study the relationship between potential difference (V) across a resistor and current (I) passing through it.

Procedure:

  1. Set up a circuit consisting of a nichrome wire XY of length 0.5m, an ammeter, a voltmeter, and four cells of 1.5V each.
  2. Connect one cell. Record reading of Ammeter (I) and Voltmeter (V).
  3. Connect two cells, three cells, and four cells successively. Record readings in each case.
  4. Calculate V/I for each pair of readings.
  5. Plot a graph of V vs I.
Observation: The ratio V/I is approximately constant. The graph is a straight line passing through the origin.
Conclusion: Potential difference is directly proportional to current (Ohm's Law verified).
Activity 11.2

Current Through Different Components

Aim: To observe that current flow depends on the nature of the electrical component.

Procedure:

  1. Take a nichrome wire, a torch bulb, and a 10W bulb.
  2. Connect an ammeter and cells in a circuit with a gap XY.
  3. Connect the nichrome wire in the gap XY. Note current.
  4. Replace with the torch bulb, then the 10W bulb. Note current in each case.
Observation: Current is different for different components connected across the same voltage source.
Conclusion: Different components offer different resistance to current flow.
Activity 11.3

Factors Affecting Resistance

Aim: To study the effect of length, area of cross-section, and material on resistance.

Procedure:

  1. Complete an electric circuit consisting of a cell, ammeter, and a gap.
  2. Connect a nichrome wire of length l (R1). Note current.
  3. Connect a nichrome wire of length 2l (same thickness). Note current.
  4. Connect a nichrome wire of length l but thicker (larger area). Note current.
  5. Connect a copper wire of length l and same area. Note current.
Observation:
  • Current decreases when length increases (Resistance increases).
  • Current increases when wire is thicker (Resistance decreases).
  • Current changes with material (Resistance depends on material nature).
Activity 11.4

Current in a Series Circuit

Aim: To check if current is the same everywhere in a series selection.

Procedure:

  1. Join three resistors of different values (e.g., 1Ω, 2Ω, 3Ω) in series.
  2. Connect an ammeter in the circuit between the battery and the first resistor. Note reading.
  3. Change the position of the ammeter to between the resistors. Note reading.
Conclusion: The reading of the ammeter remains the same at all positions. In a series combination involved, the current is constant throughout the circuit.
Activity 11.5

Potential Difference in Series Combination

Aim: To verify the potential difference distribution in series.

Procedure:

  1. Connect three resistors R1, R2, R3 in series.
  2. Measure the potential difference (V) across the combination.
  3. Measure potential differences V1, V2, and V3 across each resistor individually.
Observation: V is equal to the sum of V1, V2, and V3.
Conclusion: Total voltage is distributed across resistors in series.
Activity 11.6

Resistors in Parallel

Aim: To verify current and voltage properties in parallel combination.

Procedure:

  1. Connect three resistors in parallel to a battery.
  2. Connect a voltmeter in parallel to the combination. Note reading.
  3. Connect ammeters in series with each resistor branch to measure individual currents.
Observation:
  1. The potential difference across each resistor is the same.
  2. The total current is the sum of separate currents through each branch (I = I1 + I2 + I3).
