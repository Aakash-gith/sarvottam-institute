Activities: Magnetic Effects

Activity 12.1

Oerstedâ€™s Experiment

To show that a current-carrying wire produces a magnetic field.

  1. Take a straight thick copper wire and place it between points X and Y in an electric circuit.
  2. Place a small compass near the copper wire.
  3. Switch on the current. Observe the direction of deflection of the compass needle.
  4. Reverse the direction of current and observe again.

Observation

The compass needle is deflected on passing the current. If direction of current is reversed, the direction of deflection is also reversed.

Conclusion: Electricity and Magnetism are linked. A current-carrying conductor produces a magnetic field around it.

Activity 12.2

Magnetic Field Lines (Iron Filings)

To visualize the magnetic field around a bar magnet.

  1. Fix a sheet of white paper on a drawing board.
  2. Place a bar magnet in the center.
  3. Sprinkle some iron filings uniformly around the bar magnet.
  4. Tap the board gently.

Observation

Iron filings arrange themselves in a pattern representing the magnetic field lines. The lines are denser near the poles.

Activity 12.3

Drawing Field Lines with Compass

To draw magnetic field lines using a compass.

  1. Place a magnet on a sheet of paper. Mark its boundary.
  2. Place the compass near the North Pole of the magnet.
  3. Mark the position of two ends of the needle.
  4. Move the needle to a new position such that its south pole occupies the position previously occupied by its north pole.
  5. Repeat until you reach the South Pol. Join the points.

Observation

The path traced represents a magnetic field line. Lines emerge from North and merge at South.

Activity 12.4

Concentric Field Lines

To observe magnetic field around a straight conductor.

  1. Insert a long straight copper wire vertically through a cardboard.
  2. Connect it to a battery and key.
  3. Sprinkle iron filings on the cardboard. Switch on current and tap gently.

Observation

Filings arrange in concentric circles around the wire.

Activity 12.5

Field Direction (Right Hand Rule)

To determine the direction of the magnetic field.

  1. Use the setup from Activity 12.4.
  2. Place a compass at a point (say P) over a circle.
  3. Observe the direction of the needle.
  4. Reverse current direction.

Conclusion

If current flows vertically up, field is anticlockwise. If down, field is clockwise (Right Hand Thumb Rule).

Activity 12.6

Circular Loop Field

To observe the magnetic field of a circular coil.

  1. Pass a circular coil through a cardboard.
  2. Connect to battery. Sprinkle iron filings.

Observation

Field lines are circular near the wire. As we move towards the center, they become larger and appear straight at the center.

Activity 12.7

Force on Conductor (Kicking Wire)

To demonstrate force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.

  1. Take a small aluminium rod AB. Suspend it horizontally using connecting wires.
  2. Place a strong horseshoe magnet such that the rod lies between the poles.
  3. Pass current through the rod from B to A.

Observation

The rod gets displaced towards the left. If current is reversed, displacement is towards right.

Conclusion: A mechanical force is exerted on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field (Fleming's Left Hand Rule).
