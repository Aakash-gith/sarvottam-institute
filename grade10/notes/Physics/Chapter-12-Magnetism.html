Magnetic Field

A magnetic field is a region in space where a magnetic force can be experienced by a magnetic pole or a current-carrying conductor.

  • Magnetic Field Lines: Imaginary lines that show direction and strength of magnetic field
  • Field Lines Properties: Always form closed loops, start from North and end at South poles
  • Magnetic Induction (B): Measured in Tesla (T) or Gauss
  • Direction: Determined by right-hand rule

Lenz's Law

The direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

Statement: If the magnetic flux through a loop increases, the induced current will create a magnetic field opposing this increase. Conversely, if flux decreases, induced current opposes the decrease.

DC Motor

Converts electrical energy into mechanical energy.

Key Components:
  • Coil - carries current
  • Magnetic field - permanent magnets or electromagnets
  • Commutator - maintains torque in one direction
  • Brushes - maintain electrical contact

Eddy Currents

When a conductor moves through a changing magnetic field or vice versa, circular currents (eddy currents) are induced in the conductor.

  • Applications: Electromagnetic braking, metal detectors
  • Effect: Produces heat, causes energy loss in transformers
  • Reduction: Use laminated cores in transformers
