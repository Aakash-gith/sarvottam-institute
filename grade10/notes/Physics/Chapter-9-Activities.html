Activity 9.1

Reflection by a Spoon

Objective: To observe the nature of images formed by curved surfaces.

Procedure:

  1. Take a large shining spoon. Try to view your face in its curved surface.
  2. Do you get the image? Is it smaller or larger?
  3. Move the spoon slowly away from your face. Observe how the image changes.
  4. Reverse the spoon and repeat the activity.
Observation: The inner surface acts as a concave mirror (image inverted at distance), and the outer surface acts as a convex mirror (image always erect and diminished).
Activity 9.3

Focus of a Concave Mirror

Objective: To find the approximate focal length of a concave mirror.

Procedure:

  1. Hold a concave mirror in your hand and direct its reflecting surface towards the sun.
  2. Direct the light reflected by the mirror on to a sheet of paper held close to the mirror.
  3. Move the sheet of paper back and forth gradually until you find a bright, sharp spot of light.
  4. Measure the distance between the mirror and the paper.
Result: The bright spot is the image of the sun at the focus. The distance is the focal length. CAUTION: Do not look at the sun directly.
Activity 9.8

Refraction through a Glass Slab

Objective: To trace the path of rays of light through a glass slab.

Procedure:

  1. Fix a sheet of white paper on a drawing board. Place a rectangular glass slab in the middle.
  2. Draw the outline of the slab. Draw a normal and an incident ray.
  3. Fix two pins on the incident ray. Look through the other side of the slab and fix two more pins such that all 4 pins appear in a straight line.
  4. Remove the slab and join the points.
Observation: The emergent ray is parallel to the incident ray but laterally displaced.
Angle of Incidence (i) ≈ Angle of Emergence (e).
Activity 9.10

Image Formation by Convex Lens

Objective: To find the focal length of a convex lens.

Procedure:

  1. Take a convex lens. Find its approximate focal length by focusing sunlight (similar to Act 9.3).
  2. Draw five parallel straight lines on a table (representing O, F1, 2F1, F2, 2F2).
  3. Place the lens on 'O'. Place a burning candle at different positions (beyond 2F1, at 2F1, etc.).
  4. Obtain the image on a screen on the other side. Note the nature and size.
Conclusion: As the object moves closer to the lens (until F1), the image moves away and becomes larger.
